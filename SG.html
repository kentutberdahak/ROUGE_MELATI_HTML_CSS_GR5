Stan Getz

​Stan Getz (1927-1991) was an acclaimed American jazz saxophonist, known as "The Sound" for his distinctive, lyrical playing. Emerging in the cool jazz era, he gained widespread recognition in Woody Herman's band. In the 1960s, his collaboration with Brazilian musicians, especially on "Getz/Gilberto," popularized bossa nova worldwide. Throughout his career, Getz's smooth style and versatility made him a prominent figure in jazz until his passing in 1991.

​The Girl From Ipanema

​"The Girl from Ipanema" is a timeless bossa nova jazz standard recorded by Stan Getz and João Gilberto, featuring vocals by Astrud Gilberto. Released in 1964 as part of the album "Getz/Gilberto," the song became a global sensation. Known for its seductive melody and Astrud Gilberto's soft, English-language vocals, "The Girl from Ipanema" epitomizes the allure of bossa nova. The track achieved widespread popularity, won several Grammy Awards, and remains one of the most recognizable and beloved jazz recordings in history.

Getz/Gilberto (1964)

​"Getz/Gilberto" is a landmark jazz album released in 1964, featuring the collaborative efforts of American saxophonist Stan Getz and Brazilian guitarist João Gilberto. The album played a pivotal role in popularizing bossa nova globally. Its standout track, "The Girl from Ipanema," sung by Astrud Gilberto, became a massive hit. The album's seamless blend of American jazz and Brazilian bossa nova styles, coupled with the sultry vocals and masterful instrumental performances, earned it critical acclaim and several Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year in 1965. "Getz/Gilberto" remains a classic and a key moment in the history of jazz and Latin music fusion.

Jazz Samba Encore!(1963)

​"Jazz Samba Encore" is a 1963 album by saxophonist Stan Getz and guitarist Luiz Bonfá. A follow-up to their successful "Jazz Samba" collaboration, this album further explores the fusion of bossa nova and jazz. With its enchanting Brazilian rhythms and melodic improvisations, the album showcases the chemistry between Getz and Bonfá. While not as commercially successful as its predecessor, "Jazz Samba Encore" remains a charming and influential work, contributing to the popularization of bossa nova in the United States during the early 1960s.

Stan Getz & Bill Evans (1973)

​"Stan Getz & Bill Evans" is a collaborative jazz album released in 1964, featuring the pairing of tenor saxophonist Stan Getz and pianist Bill Evans. The album showcases a delicate and introspective musical conversation between the two jazz giants. Recorded with a rhythm section that includes bassist Ron Carter and drummer Elvin Jones, the album presents a blend of lyrical ballads and sophisticated improvisations. "Stan Getz & Bill Evans" is celebrated for its understated elegance and the seamless integration of Evans' harmonic sophistication with Getz's smooth and emotive saxophone playing.
